Scoring

For each of the three segments in a city, an athlete can earn a maximum of 1000 points. The athlete on the segment with the top time achieved in the current year receives the maximum possible score. If the segment has been ridden by 1000 or more athletes in the current year, each of the top 1000 gets one fewer point than the athlete before them, so second place gets 999, third gets 998, and so on. If there are fewer athletes than 1000, points are assigned based on a ratio of their rank versus the overall rider count, so with 500 riders, the second place athlete would get 998 points. These scores are summed up to determine the total score. In the case of ties, the athlete with the lowest cumulative time on all three segments is given the advantage.
